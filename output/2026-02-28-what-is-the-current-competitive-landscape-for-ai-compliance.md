# What is the current competitive landscape for AI compliance training platforms in Europe?

*Deepdive Report — 2026-02-28*

## Executive Summary

The EU AI Act compliance training market is rapidly segmenting into three distinct layers: (1) enterprise GRC platforms adding AI modules (Vanta, ServiceNow, NAVEX, Holistic AI), (2) specialized EU AI Act compliance tools (ComplyACT AI, artificialintelligenceact.com's AIActTPro certification), and (3) professional certification bodies (IAPP's AIGP, Coursera/Oxford). The employee-facing compliance training niche — where HverdagsAI is positioning — is surprisingly underserved. Most players focus on technical compliance documentation and risk classification (for CISOs/legal teams), NOT on training everyday employees to use AI responsibly. NAVEX is the closest direct competitor with an actual employee-facing EU AI Act course, but it's a generic US-based compliance training vendor, not AI-specialized. The Danish/Scandinavian market has no dominant local player — Danish Technological Institute offers general AI courses, and Bell Integration sells custom corporate AI training, but neither has a dedicated EU AI Act compliance training product with certification. This represents a genuine gap: companies need to train ALL employees on AI usage (Article 4 of the AI Act requires 'AI literacy'), not just their compliance officers. AIGP-certified professionals earn $141K-$170K, showing the governance talent gap is real and lucrative. The August 2026 high-risk deadline creates urgent demand.
